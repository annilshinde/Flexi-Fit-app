Ghee

Ghee is a rich source of healthy fats and can be added to various dishes to increase their caloric content.

Ghee Rice:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water

Instructions:

  • - Rinse the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain the water after soaking.
  • - In a pot, heat the ghee and add the whole spices (cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, and bay leaf). Sauté for a minute until aromatic.
  • - Add the soaked rice and sauté for a couple of minutes.
  • - Pour in the water and add salt. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook until the rice is fully cooked and fluffy.
  • - Serve the ghee rice with your choice of curry or vegetable dish.

Oats Onion Tomato

This Masala Oats recipe is a healthy and filling option, perfect for a quick breakfast or a light lunch. Feel free to add any other vegetables or spices you like to customize the flavor according to your taste preferences. Enjoy!

Masala Oats:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas (or any other vegetables of your choice)
  • 1 teaspoon oil (vegetable or olive oil)
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  • - Dry roast the rolled oats in a pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until they turn slightly golden. Set aside.
  • - In the same pan, add oil and heat it over medium heat. Add the mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  • - Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent.
  • - Stir in the chopped tomatoes and cook for a couple of minutes until they soften.
  • - Add the frozen peas (or any other vegetables you prefer) and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  • - Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • - Pour in 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Once the water comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low and add the roasted oats. Stir well to avoid lumps.
  • - Cook the oats on low heat for about 5-7 minutes, or until they are cooked and have absorbed the water. If you prefer a runny consistency, you can add more water.
  • - Garnish with fresh coriander leaves (optional) and serve hot.
